Overview of ClearCost

History

In August of 2008, the Higher Education Opportunity Act (HEOA) was signed into law, reauthorizing the Higher Education Act of 1965. In accordance with the HEOA, all postsecondary institution in the United States that receive federal student aid funds and enroll first-time, full-time undergraduate students are required to post a net price calculator on its Web site to provide current and prospective students and their families an estimated net price a student might pay to attend.Net Price is the amount that a student pays to attend an institution in a single academic year AFTER subtracting scholarships and grants the student receives. To meet this federal requirement of the HEOA, institutions may choose to use a template developed by the Department of Education, to build a net price calculator internally, or to partner with an NPC service provider. Thus, ClearCost was born, providing highly accurate and customized net price calculators to the industry.

Compliance

Minimum Required Elements

Input Elements

  • Data elements to approximate the student's Expected Family Contribution (EFC), such as income, number in family, and dependency status or factors that estimate dependency status (An institution may use either Federal Methodology or Institutional Methodology to approximate the student’s EFC.)

Output Elements

  • Estimated total cost of attendance
  • Estimated tuition and fees
  • Estimated room and board
  • Estimated books and supplies
  • Estimated other expenses (personal expenses, transportation, etc.);
  • Estimated total grant aid
  • Estimated net price
  • Percent of the cohort (full-time, first-time students) that received grant aid
  • Caveats and disclaimers, as indicated in the HEA

FAQ

Can institutions require additional information?

Institutions can require additional information beyond the minimum elements in the ED template so long as the additional elements will provide prospective students better estimates of net price. However, while an institution can ask for contact info, such as name, email address, etc., that is not related to the estimate, it cannot require it and should clearly indicate that submitting contact information is optional.

How accessible is an institution's net price calculator?

Access to an institution's net price calculator cannot be limited by requiring an application or special log in credentials

When are net price calculators updated?

Net price calculators should be updated on an annual basis when new data become available. Additionally, cost of attendance data and grant aid data should align and be from the same year.

Can institutions provide a range of estimates?

Institutions can output a range of estimates in their net price calculators but must also provide a single point estimate.

Can the Net Price Calculator be renamed?

The calculator posted to your institution’s Web site must be called “Net Price Calculator” in order to be in full statutory compliance.

How do different forms of financial aid impact the net price?

Other forms of financial aid may be present on the net price calculator but these other forms cannot impact net price.

How do VA and DOD benefits impact net price?

VA and DOD benefits are not considered need-based or merit-based grant or scholarship aid for purposes of the calculation. Therefore, institutions should not subtract these benefits from the total cost of attendance in calculating net price.
